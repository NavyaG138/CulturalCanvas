Durga Puja

Durga Puja in Kolkata is a grand, five-day festival celebrating the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with elaborate pandals, artistic idols, and cultural performances. It’s a time of joy, devotion, and community, showcasing Kolkata’s rich cultural heritage and creativity.

Rasgulla

Rasgulla is a popular Indian dessert made from soft, spongy balls of chhena (curdled milk) soaked in sugar syrup. Originating in Bengal, it's a sweet treat loved for its light, melt-in-the-mouth texture and delicate sweetness.

Howrah Bridge

Howrah Bridge, an iconic symbol of Kolkata, is a massive cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River. Opened in 1943, it connects Howrah and Kolkata without using any nuts and bolts. The bridge is a marvel of engineering and one of the busiest in the world, carrying both vehicles and pedestrians daily.

West Bengal: A Brief Historical Overview

West Bengal, located in eastern India, has a rich history characterized by influential dynasties, strategic battles, and a vibrant cultural heritage.

Key Dynasties and strongpires:

  • Ancient Period:
    • Maurya Dynasty (c. 322 BCE - 185 BCE): The region was part of the Mauryan Empire under Ashoka, who promoted Buddhism.
  • Gupta Empire (c. 320 CE - 550 CE): The Gupta Empire influenced West Bengal through trade and culture, contributing to the region's development.
  • Pala Dynasty (c. 750 CE - 1174 CE)
    • Gopala and Dharmapala: Prominent rulers known for their support of Buddhism and establishment of universities like Nalanda
  • Sena Dynasty (c. 1097 CE - 1230 CE): Known for their influence on Bengali culture and society before the Muslim invasions.
  • Mughal Empire (c. 1526 CE - 1857 CE):
    • Akbar: Established control over Bengal, making it a prosperous region within the Mughal Empire
  • British Raj (c. 1757 CE - 1947 CE):
    • Battle of Plassey (1757 CE):Marked the beginning of British control in Bengal.
    • West Bengal became a major administrative and economic center under British rule.
  • Post-Independence (1947 CE - Present):
    • West Bengal was formed as a state in independent India and has been a significant cultural and political region.

  • Battle of Plassey (1757 CE): The British East India Company defeated the Nawab of Bengal, leading to British dominance in India.
  • Battle of Buxar (1764 CE): Consolidated British control over Bengal and much of northern India.

DELHI The Heart Of Indian History

Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb, often called the "mini Taj Mahal," is where Mughal architecture found its groove, blending Persian elegance with Indian grandeur. Built in 1570, it's the first garden-tomb in India, a lush oasis in the heart of Delhi.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar: The Qutub Minar, a 73-meter-high marvel, is the world’s tallest brick minaret, built in the 13th century. It’s a symbol of victory, with intricate carvings that tell stories of a bygone era.

Red Fort

Red Fort: The Red Fort, built in 1648 by Shah Jahan, is Delhi’s majestic fortress. Its red sandstone walls have witnessed centuries of history, from Mughal splendor to India’s Independence Day celebrations.

Delhi: A Brief Historical Overview

Delhi, the capital of India, has a rich and varied history, marked by influential dynasties and significant battles.

Key Dynasties and strongpires:

  • Delhi Sultanate (c. 1206 CE - 1526 CE):
    • Mamluk Dynasty: Founded by Qutb-ud-din Aibak.
    • Khilji Dynasty: Expanded the Sultanate and implemented reforms.
    • Tughlaq Dynasty: Known for architectural achievements.
    • Sayyid and Lodi Dynasties: The final sultans before Mughal rule.
  • Mughal Empire (c. 1526 CE - 1857 CE):
    • Babur: Established Mughal rule after the Battle of Panipat.
    • Akbar: Developed Delhi as a major cultural and administrative center.
    • Shah Jahan: Built the Red Fort and expanded the city.
  • British Raj (c. 1857 CE - 1947 CE): Delhi became a major British administrative cente
  • Post-Independence (1947 CE - Present):
    • New Delhi: Became the capital of independent India

Do you know?

Red Fort was not always known by its current name? It was originally known as “Qila-e-Mubarak”. This name simplifies to “The Blessed Fort”.

Key Battles:

  • Battle of Panipat (1526 CE): Babur defeated Ibrahim Lodi, founding the Mughal Empire.
  • Battle of Delhi (1857 CE): British recaptured Delhi during the Indian Rebellion of 1857.

Cultural Significance:

Delhi’s history is defined by powerful dynasties, key battles, and its role as a cultural and political hub.

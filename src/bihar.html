Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is a four-day festival dedicated to the Sun God, celebrated mainly in Bihar. Devotees fast and offer prayers to the setting and rising sun, seeking health and prosperity. It’s a vibrant, community-focused celebration that highlights spiritual devotion and nature’s connection..

Nalanda University

TNalanda University, established in the 5th century CE in Bihar, was one of the world's first residential universities. Renowned for its vast library and diverse subjects, it attracted scholars from all over Asia. Destroyed in the 12th century, it remains a symbol of ancient India’s academic excellence..

Madhubani Painting

Madhubani painting, originating from Bihar, is a traditional Indian art form known for its intricate patterns and vibrant colors. Typically depicting nature, mythology, and daily life, these paintings are created using natural dyes and are often done on walls, cloth, or paper. It’s a beautiful expression of folk culture and storytelling..

Bihar: A Brief Historical Overview

Bihar, a state in eastern India, has been a pivotal center of political, religious, and cultural developments throughout history.

Key Dynasties and strongpires:

  • Magadha Kingdom (6th century BCE - 4th century BCE):
    • Haryanka Dynasty: Early rulers like Bimbisara and Ajatashatru.
    • Nanda Dynasty: Known for its wealth and military strength.
  • Maurya Dynasty (c. 322 BCE - 185 BCE):
    • Chandragupta Maurya: Unified much of India.
    • Ashoka: Expanded the empire and promoted Buddhism, with significant edicts and stupas.
  • Gupta Dynasty (c. 320 CE - 550 CE): Known for the "Golden Age" of India, marked by advancements in science, art, and literature.
  • Pala Dynasty (c. 750 CE - 1174 CE):
    • Gopala and Dharmapala: Promoted Buddhism and supported universities like Nalanda.
  • Mughal Empire (c. 1526 CE - 1857 CE): Bihar was part of the Mughal Empire, with cities like Patna becoming significant.
  • British Raj (c. 1858 CE - 1947 CE): Bihar played a role in India’s independence struggle.
